CLIx in the Classroom

Student modules use a combination of digital and non-digital tools and low cost, locally available materials for lab activities to enable students to be creators, and not merely consumers of content. The topics of the modules are guided by the National Curriculum Framework 2005, mapped to the state curriculum and selected based on hard-spots. Guided by the philosophies of constructivism and constructionism, CLIxOERs offer students opportunities to work in a hands-on manner to construct knowledge in active ways. Relevant research literature relating to the topics were drawn upon while creating the contents. Feedback was sought from teacher educators and subject experts from state governments before the contents were authored and reviewed multiple times for Quality Assurance. This is supported by feedback from teachers in real time, which is a paradigmatic shift from trends where technology is used to either teacher-proof curricula, or micro-manage teachers.
All CLIx Open Educational Resources (OERs) are released under the Creative Commons Licence 4.0. so that these resources can be reused, re-created, modified and adapted to varied contexts. It aims, in the process, to be truly free - where we as the spirit of freedom and independence to create, learn and grow!

We encourage teachers to innovate and come up with new strategies to contextualise these OERs and integrate in their everyday teaching-learning process.
